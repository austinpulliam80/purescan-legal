What PureScan Analyzes

Every scan tells the
full story.

Our AI engine breaks down every ingredient with scientific reasoning, regulatory context, and personalized risk assessment.

🔬
Ingredient Risk Breakdown
Every ingredient classified as No Risk, Negligible, Low, Moderate, or High — with scientific rationale and E-number references.
🧪
Harmful Additive Detection
Automatically flags additives red-listed or under review by the FDA, EFSA, FSA, or Health Canada depending on your region.
🌿
Seed Oil Scanner
Detects canola, soybean, sunflower, corn, safflower, grapeseed, and cottonseed oils — a critical flag for health-conscious users.
⚠️
Heavy Metal Risk Assessment
Estimates potential exposure to Lead, Cadmium, Arsenic, and Mercury — calibrated against FDA action levels and EU standards.
📦
Packaging Risk Analysis
Material type, BPA concerns, microplastic leaching potential, recyclability grade, and environmental impact summary.
🏢
Conglomerate Ownership
Transparent parent-company ownership chain so you know exactly who makes what you're buying — powered by Wikidata.
🍽️
NOVA Processing Level
Classifies every product on the NOVA scale (1–4), from whole foods to ultra-processed — a key global nutrition research standard.
✅
Safer Alternatives
Every scan surfaces 2–3 real-world product alternatives with higher safety scores when your scanned item scores poorly.
🎯
Personalized Profile
Set your dietary preferences, allergens, region, and product categories — and every scan is calibrated specifically for you.
Concern System

Instant visual clarity
at a glance.

PureScan's color-coded concern system makes risk immediately understandable — no decoding required.

Low Concern
Product meets clean standards — ingredients are generally recognized as safe with no significant flags for your profile.
Medium Concern
Some additives or processing methods warrant attention. Use with awareness depending on your sensitivities.
High Concern
Contains ingredients flagged by major regulatory bodies or our AI as posing notable health or safety concerns.
Unknown
Insufficient data to assess — product information may be incomplete or the ingredient list could not be verified.

safety standards.
